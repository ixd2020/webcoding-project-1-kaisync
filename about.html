ABOUT THE ARTIST





"I paint my own reality. The only thing I know is that I paint because I need to, and I paint whatever passes through my head without any other consideration."

FRIDA KAHLO

was born in July 6th, 1907 in Coyaocan, Mexico. Her life evolved during a historic tme in Mexico when the struggle for social change began to gain significance.

Having overcame a severe case of polio in her early childhood and having to continue her life with the physical effects from the disease, and also being one of the thirty five women of the first generation who attended the National Preparatory School, Kahlo showed great personality and devotion to her life.

In 1925, Kahlo was traveling on a bus when it crashed into a tramcar, severely affecting her life - it was her second close encounter with death. Spending several months with limited mobility and learning to cope with the boredom of her recover process, Kahlo soon began to paint with the water-colours of her father, the talented photographer Guillermo Kahlo. Thus her interest in becoming an artist grew.

Kahlo met fellow Mexican artist Diego Rivera Mexican Communist Party in 1927 due to her interest in politics and art. The two artists got married in 1929, and spent the late 1920s and early 1930s travelling in Mexico and the United States.

In this time, Kahlo developed her artistic style by drawing inspiration from Mexican folk culture. She painted small self-portraits with elements from pre-Columbian and Catholic beliefs. The work of Kahlo caught the attention of the Surrealist artist André Breton who arranged for her first solo exhibition in New York in 1938.
